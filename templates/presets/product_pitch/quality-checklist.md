# Product Pitch Quality Checklist

## Narrative

- [ ] The cover contains a sharp product promise, not a generic category claim.
- [ ] The problem slide describes a concrete user workflow.
- [ ] The solution slide shows what changes for the user.
- [ ] The ask is specific and appears before the closing visual.

## Proof

- [ ] Demo or workflow pages show actual states, screens, artifacts, or clear diagrams.
- [ ] Traction, roadmap, and market claims are sourced or labeled as assumptions.
- [ ] The pitch does not rely on vague "AI-powered" language without proof.

## Visual Quality

- [ ] Web Deck alternates dense information and breathing slides.
- [ ] Images support the product story and are not purely atmospheric.
- [ ] Mobile preview remains readable.
- [ ] Navigation and first viewport communicate the product immediately.

## Optional PPTX

- [ ] Summary, market, roadmap, and appendix pages remain editable.
- [ ] Speaker notes explain demo flow and assumptions.

## Delivery

- [ ] Final response lists generated files and checks performed.
- [ ] Public demo material contains no private source data.
