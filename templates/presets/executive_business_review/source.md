# Quarterly Business Review

## Audience
Executive team, business owners, and project leads.

## Core Question
Did this period's growth improve the quality of the business, and what actions should the team prioritize next?

## One-Line Answer
Revenue continued to grow, but quality diverged by region and channel; the next cycle should shift from broad expansion to focused owner-led action on retention, high-margin channels, and risk controls.

## KPI Snapshot

| Metric | Current | Target | Previous | Note |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---|
| Revenue growth | 18% | 15% | 12% | Growth exceeded target. |
| Gross margin | 42% | 45% | 44% | Channel mix reduced margin. |
| Renewal rate | 81% | 86% | 84% | Needs retention action. |
| New strategic accounts | 23 | 20 | 17 | Healthy enterprise expansion. |
| Delivery SLA | 94% | 96% | 95% | Service pressure remains. |

## Performance Variance

- East region beat revenue target by 9 points because enterprise accounts closed earlier than expected.
- South region missed margin target because discounting increased in channel-led deals.
- Renewal rate declined in mid-market accounts with slow onboarding.
- Strategic account growth is healthy but increases implementation load.

## Drivers

1. Enterprise pipeline quality improved after vertical solution packaging.
2. Channel partners brought faster volume but weaker margin control.
3. Product onboarding gaps delayed value realization for mid-market customers.
4. Support workload grew faster than delivery staffing.

## Risks And Opportunities

- Risk: Renewal decline may offset new logo growth in the next two quarters.
- Risk: Discounting habits may become normalized without approval gates.
- Opportunity: Enterprise vertical playbook can be replicated in two more regions.
- Opportunity: Onboarding automation can reduce support load and improve time-to-value.

## Decisions Needed

- Whether to cap channel discount authority next quarter.
- Whether to fund onboarding automation before expanding into two new regions.
- Whether to reassign delivery capacity to strategic accounts.

## Next Actions
